# QA Checklist

## Must Pass

- It is a 16:9 landscape image.
- One image explains one core idea only.
- The image is readable quickly.
- The composition uses deliberate whitespace.
- The labels are sparse, short, and readable.
- The active preset is visible in the mood and object vocabulary.
- If the mascot appears, the mascot performs the core action and still matches the bundled reference image.
- The image does not feel like a PPT slide, dense architecture board, or dashboard UI.
- The scene invents a metaphor for the current article instead of falling back to generic hacker wallpaper cues.

## Failure Signals

If any of these appear, regenerate or edit:

- the mascot feels decorative
- the illustration drifts into glossy sci-fi poster art
- the image depends on too many screens, panels, overlays, or neat boxes
- the text becomes a paragraph
- the visual metaphor is too generic or not specific to the article
- the composition is too crowded or arrow-heavy
- the style is loud but the explanation is weak

## Iteration Methods

- Too ordinary: swap the main object family and make the action more physical
- Too UI-like: remove screens, frames, and panel borders; replace them with tactile objects
- Too dense: reduce nodes and labels, then keep only one dominant action
- Too character-driven: reduce mascot size or switch to `minimal mascot`
- Too generic cyberpunk: use fewer neon-like cues and more low-tech tools, cables, locks, notes, and maintenance objects

## Delivery Standard

The best result should make the reader think "interesting" first and "I get it" immediately after. If it feels like a slide template with hacker decorations, it is not good enough.
